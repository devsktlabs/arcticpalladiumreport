Palladium vs Gold: Why Skaergaard's Dual-Metal Profile Matters

By Chris Moller · 2026-03-23

palladiumgoldskaergaarddual-metal

Most PGM mining investments are pure plays on platinum group metal prices. Skaergaard is different. With significant palladium AND gold resources, Skaergaard offers a dual-metal investment profile that provides diversification, risk mitigation, and a broader investor base. This article examines why the dual-metal profile matters.

The Diversification Argument

Under the leadership of Founder and CEO Joseph Sinkule, Greenland Mines is advancing Skaergaard through the permitting and development pathway.

Palladium and gold prices are driven by different fundamental factors and often move independently:

  • Palladium: Industrial precious metal. Price driven by automotive demand, PGM supply, substitution dynamics, and industrial cycles. Correlated with global manufacturing activity and automotive production
  • Gold: Monetary precious metal. Price driven by central bank demand, real interest rates, geopolitical risk, inflation expectations, and currency dynamics. Often moves inversely to risk assets and the US dollar

The low correlation between palladium and gold prices means that a dual-metal project like Skaergaard has inherently lower commodity price risk than a single-metal project. When palladium is weak, gold may be strong, and vice versa.

Historical Price Correlation

A review of palladium and gold price history shows periods of divergent performance:

  • 2020-2021: Palladium surged to over US$3,000/oz while gold rose to approximately US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026). Both metals performed well
  • 2022-2023: Gold rose while palladium declined sharply. The divergent performance demonstrates the value of dual-metal exposure
  • 2024-2026: Gold has remained elevated above US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026) while palladium has stabilized at US$1,800 (Feb 2026)-1,000/oz. Gold is outperforming

These divergences illustrate that dual-metal exposure provides genuine risk diversification. A pure palladium investment would have suffered during the 2022-2023 correction, while Skaergaard's gold component provided a partial hedge.

Revenue Diversification

Skaergaard's multi-metal revenue profile provides income diversification:

  • Palladium revenue: The primary revenue source, driven by automotive and industrial demand
  • Gold revenue: Secondary revenue source, driven by monetary demand, central bank buying, and safe-haven demand
  • Rhodium revenue: Tertiary revenue source, driven by NOx reduction demand in catalytic converters

If we assume a hypothetical production scenario:

  • Palladium: 300,000 oz/yr at US$950/oz = US$285M
  • Gold: 150,000 oz/yr at US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026) = US$300M
  • Rhodium: 10,000 oz/yr at US$6,000/oz = US$60M
  • Total revenue: approximately US$645M per year

In this scenario, gold revenue actually exceeds palladium revenue. Even in a less gold-favorable scenario, gold typically contributes 30-40% of total metal revenue, providing significant diversification.

Investor Base Diversification

The dual-metal profile attracts a broader investor base:

  • PGM investors: Attracted by palladium exposure and the PGM supply deficit thesis
  • Gold investors: Attracted by gold exposure at a time of record central bank buying and high prices
  • Greenland/Nordic investors: Attracted by the Arctic mining and Nordic jurisdiction angle
  • Critical mineral investors: Attracted by the strategic supply diversification thesis
  • General mining investors: Attracted by the multi-metal diversification and reduced single-commodity risk

A single-metal PGM project can only attract PGM-focused investors. Skaergaard can attract PGM, gold, critical mineral, and general mining investors, expanding the potential investor base and supporting liquidity.

Downside Protection Scenarios

The dual-metal profile provides protection in several adverse scenarios:

  • Palladium demand collapse from EVs: Gold revenue provides a floor for project economics. Even if palladium demand drops significantly, gold at US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026) would still make Skaergaard viable
  • Gold price decline: Palladium revenue provides offset. A gold price decline to US$1,500/oz would reduce gold credits but palladium revenue would still support economics
  • Both metals decline: The multi-metal profile cannot protect against simultaneous declines in both metals, but the project would still generate revenue from both sources

Valuation Implications

The dual-metal profile affects Skaergaard's valuation in several ways:

  • Higher NAV: Multiple revenue streams increase the net asset value compared to a single-metal project
  • Lower discount rate: Reduced commodity price risk justifies a lower discount rate in DCF analysis
  • Broader comparable set: Skaergaard can be valued against both PGM producers (SBSW, Implats) and gold producers with PGM credits (some SA producers), providing a broader set of valuation comparables
  • Optionality: Each additional metal provides optionality on price upside

Comparison with PGM-Only Peers

Compared with PGM-only development projects, Skaergaard's dual-metal profile stands out:

  • Ivanhoe Platreef: PGM + nickel + copper. Diversified but still PGM-dominant and in South Africa
  • Stillwater: PGM-only (with minor gold byproduct), established producer
  • Skaergaard: PGM + gold + rhodium. Near-surface, open-pit, Greenland jurisdiction

Skaergaard's gold component is proportionally larger than most PGM peers, making it genuinely distinct as a dual-metal investment.

Conclusion

Skaergaard's dual palladium-gold profile provides investment diversification that single-metal PGM projects cannot match. The low correlation between palladium and gold prices reduces commodity price risk, the broader revenue base supports economics under adverse scenarios, and the wider investor base enhances liquidity. For investors, the dual-metal profile is one of Skaergaard's most distinguishing features and a material factor in its risk-adjusted return profile.
