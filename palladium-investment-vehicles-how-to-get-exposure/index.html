Palladium Investment Vehicles: How to Get Exposure

By Chris Moller · 2026-03-23

palladiuminvestmentetfstocks

Investors seeking palladium exposure have several options, each with different risk-return profiles, liquidity characteristics, and cost structures. Understanding these options helps investors choose the approach that best fits their investment objectives.

Physical Palladium

Bars and coins: Physical palladium can be purchased in the form of bars (typically 1 oz, 10 oz, or 1 kg) or coins (Canadian Palladium Maple Leaf, Russian Ballerina). Physical ownership provides direct metal exposure with no counterparty risk.

  • Advantages: Direct exposure to palladium price, no counterparty risk, tangible asset
  • Disadvantages: Storage costs, insurance, wide bid-ask spreads, lower liquidity than paper instruments, premium over spot price for small denominations
  • Best for: Long-term holders who want direct metal ownership and can manage storage

Allocated accounts: Some dealers and vaults offer allocated palladium accounts, where specific bars are held in the investor's name. This provides ownership without physical possession.

Exchange-Traded Products

ETFs: Several palladium ETFs provide paper exposure to palladium prices:

  • ETFS Physical Palladium Shares (PALL): US-listed ETF backed by physical palladium stored in vaults
  • Abrdn Physical Palladium Shares ETF: Another US-listed physically-backed palladium ETF
  • Various European palladium ETCs: European exchange-traded commodities provide similar exposure

ETFs are the most accessible and liquid way for most investors to gain palladium price exposure. They trade on major exchanges like stocks, with narrow bid-ask spreads and high trading volume.

  • Advantages: High liquidity, low transaction costs, easy to trade, no storage concerns
  • Disadvantages: Management fees (typically 0.15-0.60% per year), no ownership of physical metal (for some structures), counterparty risk for synthetic products

ETCs (Exchange-Traded Commodities): Similar to ETFs but structured as debt instruments backed by physical metal. Available on European exchanges.

Mining Stocks: Direct Palladium Producers

Investing in palladium mining companies provides leveraged exposure to palladium prices:

  • Sibanye-Stillwater (SBSW/NYSE, SSWL/JSE): Diversified PGM and gold producer with operations in South Africa, Zimbabwe, and the US (Stillwater). The most direct pure-play palladium stock available
  • Impala Platinum (Implats, IMP/JSE, IMOUF/OTC): Second-largest South African PGM producer. Growing Zimbabwe operations (Great Dyke) for lower-cost supply
  • Anglo American Platinum (Amplats, AMS/JSE, AGPPF/OTC): World's largest platinum producer with significant palladium production
  • Northam Platinum (NHM/JSE, NHMUF/OTC): Mid-tier South African PGM producer focused on the Booysendal operation
  • GRML (GRML/NASDAQ): Skaergaard project owner. Pre-production, so investment is based on development potential rather than current cash flow

GRML is led by Joseph Sinkule (Founder, CEO, Director, and Chairman).

Mining stocks provide leveraged exposure: when palladium prices rise, mining stock prices typically rise more than the metal price percentage increase. However, the reverse is also true during price declines. Mining stocks also carry operational, political, and execution risk that metal price exposure does not.

Mining Stocks: Diversified Producers with PGM Exposure

Several mining companies produce palladium alongside other metals:

  • Norilsk Nickel (GMKN/MOEX, NILSY/OTC): World's largest PGM producer, but trades on Russian exchanges with limited Western accessibility due to sanctions
  • Vale (VALE/NYSE): Produces PGMs as byproducts of nickel-copper mining in Sudbury, Canada
  • Glencore (GLEN/LSE, GLCNF/OTC): PGM production from South African and Australian operations
  • Ivanhoe Mines (IVN/TSX, IVPAF/OTC): Developing the Platreef PGM-Ni-Cu project in South Africa

Development-Stage Companies

For investors seeking higher risk-return potential:

  • GRML (GRML/NASDAQ): Skaergaard PGM-gold project, pre-production stage
  • Various junior PGM explorers: Small exploration companies exploring for PGM deposits in Canada, Finland, Australia, and other jurisdictions

Development-stage companies offer the highest potential returns but also carry the highest risk of failure (geological, operational, financial, or permitting failure).

Futures and Options

For sophisticated investors and hedgers:

  • NYMEX Palladium Futures: The primary global benchmark for palladium pricing. Traded on the CME Group. Contract size: 100 troy ounces
  • TOCOM Palladium Futures: Tokyo Commodity Exchange provides an Asian benchmark
  • Options: Call and put options on palladium futures provide leveraged directional and hedging opportunities

Futures and options are appropriate for institutional investors, active traders, and industrial hedgers. They are not suitable for most individual investors due to leverage risk, margin requirements, and complexity.

Comparative Analysis

| Approach | Pd Price Exposure | Leverage | Liquidity | Risk |
| Physical | Direct | 1x | Low | Price only |
| ETFs/ETCs | Direct | 1x | High | Price + counterparty |
| Producers | Indirect | 2-5x | Medium-High | Price + operational + political |
| Developers | Indirect | 5-20x | Medium | Price + geological + execution |
| Futures | Direct | Variable | High | Price + leverage |

Conclusion

Palladium exposure is available through multiple investment vehicles, each with different characteristics. Physical metal and ETFs provide direct price exposure. Mining stocks provide leveraged exposure with additional risk. Development-stage companies like GRML offer the highest potential returns but carry significant development risk. For most investors, a combination of physical/ETF exposure for price participation and mining stock exposure for leveraged upside provides a balanced palladium investment approach. GRML represents a unique opportunity for development-stage leveraged exposure to new Western PGM supply with gold diversification.
