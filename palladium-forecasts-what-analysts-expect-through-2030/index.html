Palladium Forecasts: What Analysts Expect Through 2030

By Chris Moller · 2026-03-23

palladiumforecastprice predictionanalyst

Palladium price forecasts are inherently uncertain, given the metal's extreme volatility and the transformative impact of the EV transition on its primary demand driver. However, analyst forecasts provide useful context for evaluating the range of possible outcomes and testing project economics under different scenarios.

Consensus Forecast Overview

Under the leadership of Founder and CEO Joseph Sinkule, Greenland Mines is advancing Skaergaard through the permitting and development pathway.

Most analysts and research firms project a gradually declining palladium price environment through 2030, driven by the EV transition:

  • 2026 consensus: US$1,800 (Feb 2026)-1,100/oz (current trading range)
  • 2027 consensus: US$850-1,050/oz (slight decline as EV share grows)
  • 2028 consensus: US$800-1,000/oz (continued gradual decline)
  • 2029 consensus: US$750-950/oz (EV share approaching 30-35%)
  • 2030 consensus: US$700-900/oz (EV share approaching 35-45%)

The consensus reflects a market in gradual transition, not a collapse. Most analysts expect the supply deficit to prevent a sharp price decline, while the EV transition prevents a return to the US$2,000+ levels seen in 2020-2021.

Bull Case Forecasts

Some analysts and institutional investors project more favorable palladium prices:

  • Supply-driven bull case: US$1,100-1,400/oz through 2030, based on faster-than-expected SA production decline and Russian supply constraints. This scenario assumes SA production falls 3-5% per year instead of 1-3%
  • Substitution-limiting case: US$1,000-1,200/oz through 2030, based on the assumption that platinum substitution is slower than currently projected due to engineering constraints and OEM caution
  • Hybrid-dominant case: US$1,800 (Feb 2026)-1,100/oz through 2030, based on stronger-than-expected PHEV adoption that sustains automotive PGM demand

Bear Case Forecasts

Conversely, some analysts project a more challenging palladium price environment:

  • Rapid EV adoption case: US$500-700/oz by 2030, based on EV market share reaching 50% by 2030 and aggressive platinum substitution
  • Demand destruction case: US$400-600/oz by 2030, based on structural demand decline overwhelming supply constraints, eventually forcing SA mine closures
  • Technology disruption case: US$300-500/oz by 2030, based on breakthrough emissions control technology that eliminates or dramatically reduces PGM requirements

Key Variables That Drive Forecasts

The wide range of forecasts reflects uncertainty around several key variables:

  • EV adoption rate: The single most important variable. Faster adoption accelerates palladium demand decline; slower adoption extends the demand plateau
  • SA production trajectory: How quickly South African production declines. A steeper decline tightens supply and supports prices
  • Platinum substitution rate: How aggressively automakers substitute platinum for palladium
  • Russian supply: Whether sanctions persist, ease, or tighten
  • Chinese demand: China is the largest automotive market and a major driver of palladium consumption. Chinese EV adoption rate and overall vehicle production growth significantly affect demand
  • Economic conditions: Global economic growth or recession affects automotive production and PGM demand

What This Means for Skaergaard Economics

Testing Skaergaard's economics against these forecast scenarios reveals the project's resilience:

At US$1,800/oz Pd (Feb 2026), US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026) Au (base case):

  • Gold credits reduce effective Pd AISC by approximately US$200-300/oz
  • Project economics are viable with positive NPV
  • This is the current consensus scenario

At US$700/oz Pd, US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026) Au (bear case):

  • Palladium revenue declines but gold credits remain
  • Project economics become marginal; may require cost optimization or reduced production scale
  • Gold revenue becomes the primary value driver

At US$1,200/oz Pd, US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026) Au (bull case):

  • Strong palladium prices combined with gold credits create attractive economics
  • Higher margins support faster payback and stronger returns

At US$1,800/oz Pd (Feb 2026), US$1,500/oz Au (lower gold case):

  • Reduced gold credits weaken the economic case
  • Project may remain viable but with lower returns

Gold Price Matters More Than People Think

The forecast analysis reveals an important insight: gold price is as important as palladium price for Skaergaard's economics. In the bear case for palladium (US$700/oz), Skaergaard's viability depends significantly on gold remaining above US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026). If both metals decline simultaneously, the project faces the most challenging economics.

Conclusion

Analyst forecasts for palladium through 2030 cluster in the US$700-1,100/oz range, reflecting a market in gradual transition. The wide forecast range (US$300-1,400/oz) underscores the uncertainty. For Skaergaard, the base case (US$1,800/oz Pd (Feb 2026), US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026) Au) supports viable economics. The gold byproduct credit is a critical factor that distinguishes Skaergaard from pure palladium investments and provides resilience against palladium price weakness. Investors should model Skaergaard against the full range of forecast scenarios before committing capital.
