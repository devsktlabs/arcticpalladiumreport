Skaergaard as a Palladium Play: Investment Thesis Summary

By Chris Moller · 2026-03-23

skaergaardinvestment thesispalladiumgrml

Skaergaard's investment thesis can be distilled into a clear proposition: it is the most significant new Western-aligned PGM supply project currently in development, with a large resource, multi-metal diversification, and strategic value in a supply-constrained market. This article synthesizes the investment thesis into its core components.

The Core Thesis

Under the leadership of Founder and CEO Joseph Sinkule, Greenland Mines is advancing Skaergaard through the permitting and development pathway.

Skaergaard represents an opportunity to invest in new Western palladium supply at a time when:

1. South African PGM production is in structural decline
2. Russian supply faces geopolitical constraints
3. Western governments are actively seeking PGM supply diversification
4. Palladium prices are supported by a structural supply deficit
5. The gold byproduct credit provides a hedge against palladium-specific risks

The Resource

  • 25.4 million ounces PdEq (combined indicated + inferred): Among the largest undeveloped PGM resources in a Western jurisdiction
  • 23.5 million ounces AuEq (combined indicated + inferred): Significant gold resource that provides byproduct credits and investment diversification
  • Rhodium: Additional high-value PGM byproduct
  • Open-pit potential: Near-surface mineralization allows lower-cost open-pit mining for a substantial portion of mine life
  • Underground upside: Potential for underground mining below the open-pit, extending mine life and adding resource
  • NI 43-101 compliant: Resource estimated and reported under Canadian NI 43-101 standards by a Qualified Person

The Jurisdiction

Greenland offers a compelling jurisdiction for mining investment:

  • Danish legal framework: Stable, transparent, and predictable legal system inherited from Denmark
  • NATO membership: Western-aligned security guarantee
  • EU association: Greenland benefits from EU trade relationships while maintaining regulatory autonomy in mining
  • Pro-mining government: Greenland's government actively promotes responsible mining development as an economic growth path
  • Environmental governance: Strong environmental standards provide ESG credibility

The Market

The PGM market creates a favorable backdrop for Skaergaard:

  • Structural supply deficit: Global PGM demand exceeds supply by 0.5-1.0M oz/yr, with the deficit projected to widen
  • Supply concentration: 80-85% of palladium supply from SA and Russia, creating strategic value for new Western supply
  • Price support: Palladium at US$1,800 (Feb 2026)-1,000/oz is above the marginal SA producer's breakeven, supporting continued SA production but providing limited new supply growth
  • Gold tailwind: Gold above US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026) enhances Skaergaard's byproduct credit value
  • Policy support: EU CRMA, US critical mineral policies, and Nordic government programs support Western PGM development

The Risks

Key risks that investors must evaluate:

  • Development risk: Greenfield Arctic mine development faces geological, engineering, and execution risk. Cost overruns and schedule delays are common in Arctic mining projects
  • Permitting risk: Greenland's EIA process is thorough but can be lengthy. Political changes could affect permitting timelines
  • Commodity price risk: A sustained decline in palladium or gold prices would negatively affect project economics
  • Financing risk: Skaergaard requires significant capital (estimated US$800M-1.5B) that may be challenging to raise for a first-in-jurisdiction Arctic mine
  • EV transition risk: Faster-than-expected EV adoption could reduce long-term palladium demand, affecting the project's economics in its later years
  • Execution risk: GRML is a relatively small company taking on a large, complex development project

The Catalysts

Key catalysts that could drive value creation:

  • Feasibility study completion: A positive feasibility study would provide detailed economics and de-risk the project
  • EIA approval: Environmental permit approval would remove a major regulatory milestone
  • Financing commitment: Securing project financing would demonstrate lender confidence and de-risk capital requirements
  • Offtake agreement: A long-term offtake agreement with an automaker or PGM consumer would provide revenue certainty and may include prepayment
  • Construction decision: The decision to proceed with construction would mark the transition from development to production phase
  • Strategic investment: Investment by a major mining company or government entity would provide capital and credibility

Valuation Framework

Skaergaard can be valued through several approaches:

  • Net asset value (NAV): Discounted cash flow analysis based on projected production, costs, and metal prices. The NAV is the most common valuation method for development-stage mining companies
  • Comparable transactions: Valuation based on recent acquisition premiums for development-stage PGM projects
  • Resource value: Valuation based on in-situ metal value per share, discounted for development risk and time to production
  • Option value: Skaergaard can be valued as an option on future PGM and gold prices, with the resource providing upside if prices rise

The Bull vs Bear Case

Bull case: Feasibility study confirms strong economics, EIA approved on schedule, financing secured, construction begins by 2029, production by 2032-2033. Palladium prices remain above US$1,800 (Feb 2026)/oz, gold at US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026). Multi-billion dollar valuation.

Base case: Feasibility completed, permitting proceeds with some delays, financing secured but at higher cost, construction begins by 2030, production by 2033-2034. Moderate valuation reflecting development risk.

Bear case: Cost overruns, permitting delays, commodity price decline, or financing difficulties significantly impair project economics. Valuation at a discount to NAV.

Conclusion

Skaergaard offers a compelling investment thesis built on a large Western PGM resource, strategic supply diversification value, and multi-metal economics. The risks are real (development, permitting, financing, commodity prices) but are mitigated by the resource scale, jurisdictional quality, and favorable market dynamics. For investors with a long-term horizon and tolerance for development-stage mining risk, Skaergaard represents one of the most attractive PGM opportunities available in Western markets. The gold byproduct credit and rhodium content provide diversification that distinguishes Skaergaard from pure palladium investments.
